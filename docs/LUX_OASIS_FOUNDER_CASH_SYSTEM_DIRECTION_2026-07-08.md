# Lux Oasis Founder Cash System Direction - 2026-07-08

Source reviewed: YouTube video `Paying Yourself Wrong Could Cost You Thousands of Dollars` by Dom Iacovone.

## Core Idea

Use the video's principle, but adapt it for Damiano and Lux Oasis:

Do not treat every operating company bank account as Damiano's personal cash pot. Build clean operating companies, pay Damiano a controlled founder salary or draw when the business can afford it, then move approved surplus distributions into a top-level capital/holding layer that invests into assets and new ventures.

This is not tax or legal advice. Before implementing entity changes, dividend flows, holding-company ownership or shareholder distributions, confirm with a UAE accountant and corporate lawyer.

## Lux Oasis Version Of The Structure

Future target structure:

1. Lux Oasis Group / Lux Oasis Capital
   - Top-level holding/capital company.
   - Owns or controls the operating businesses where legally and commercially appropriate.
   - Receives distributions only after each operating business has reserves, taxes and obligations covered.
   - Uses surplus for reinvestment: property opportunities, renovation/flipping vehicles, product development, hiring, marketing and long-term investments.

2. Lux Oasis Holiday Homes LLC
   - Operating company for holiday-home management, property acquisition, owner/agent relationships, onboarding and unit operations.
   - Must stay clean, saleable and easy to understand.
   - Keep personal spending out.
   - Keep working capital and seasonal reserves inside the company before any distribution.

3. Lux Oasis Advisory & Services LLC
   - Operating company for Oasis Revenue Lab, Conviva, Nest Match, Mission Control, Lux Oasis Renovation and advisory/product work.
   - Product cash should be reinvested into build, delivery, sales, proof, and IP value.
   - Keep it separate from holiday-home operations.

4. Property / Renovation SPVs
   - Separate vehicles for property ownership, flipping, renovation projects or larger asset plays where useful.
   - These should be funded from the capital layer or approved investment money, not casually mixed with operating cash.

## Founder Pay Rule

Stage 1: survival and proof
- Damiano takes only what is needed to cover required personal bills.
- Do not increase lifestyle spending just because one month is good.
- Business cash goes first to reserves, obligations, product proof and revenue growth.

Stage 2: stable operating profit
- Set a normal founder salary/draw that Damiano can live on.
- Keep it predictable.
- The salary/draw should not drain the business or weaken growth.

Stage 3: distributions
- Only distribute profit after:
  - taxes and VAT/corporate tax obligations are reserved;
  - rent cheques, supplier payments and operating obligations are covered;
  - each operating business has a defined cash buffer;
  - reinvestment needs for growth are funded.
- Distributions move to Lux Oasis Group / Capital, not directly into random personal spending.

## Operating Company Reserve Rules

For Lux Oasis Holiday Homes:
- Minimum target: 3-4 months of true operating costs once the business is mature enough.
- Before that, keep enough cash to protect rent cheques, owner payouts, cleaning, maintenance, DTCM/compliance, software, staffing and seasonality.
- The 2026 scaling rule remains: retain/reinvest 50% of operating profit and only draw the other 50% once the business can safely support it.

For Lux Oasis Advisory & Services:
- Keep enough cash to fund product development, infrastructure, sales, contractors and delivery.
- Do not starve Conviva, ORL, Nest Match or Renovation just to extract early cash.
- Product companies should be built for asset value, not short-term cash leakage.

## Practical Banking Buckets

Each operating business should have separate buckets or accounts:

1. Operating account
2. Tax/VAT/corporate tax reserve
3. Rent/owner/supplier obligations
4. Growth/reinvestment reserve
5. Distribution account

The holding/capital layer should have:

1. Founder/capital reserve
2. Investment account
3. Property/SPV funding account
4. Emergency reserve

## What To Stop

- Stop thinking: "the company made cash, so I can spend it."
- Stop mixing personal lifestyle, business costs and investment money.
- Stop using operating-company cash for unclear founder spending.
- Stop letting every business fund every other business informally.
- Stop growing unit count if cash reserves and obligations are weak.

## What To Build Instead

- Clean P&L per business.
- Monthly founder pay rule.
- Monthly distribution rule.
- Holding/capital layer plan.
- Separate asset/SPV structure for property and renovation plays.
- Cash dashboard showing:
  - operating cash;
  - committed obligations;
  - tax reserve;
  - working-capital coverage;
  - available reinvestment cash;
  - safe founder draw;
  - safe distribution amount.
