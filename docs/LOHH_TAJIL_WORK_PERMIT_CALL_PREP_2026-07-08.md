## Official Baseline To Mention

Official UAE/MOHRE sources show:

- UAE private-sector work generally requires a work permit before someone undertakes work for an employer.
- MOHRE lists work patterns including full-time, part-time, temporary work, flexible work, remote work and job sharing.
- Temporary work permits are for a specific task or period.
- Part-time permits may require the worker's current employer NOC if they already work for another employer.
- Probation exists under employment contracts but is still employment, not a way to work without a permit.
- UAE Government guidance says probation must not exceed six months.
- Free-zone employees are generally subject to the rules of their free-zone authority, so SHAMS/free-zone status may change the exact channel and documents.
