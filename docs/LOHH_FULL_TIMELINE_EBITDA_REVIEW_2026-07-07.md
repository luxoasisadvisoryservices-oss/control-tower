# LOHH Full Timeline EBITDA Review - 2026-07-07

Source file: `LUX_OASIS_Holiday_Homes_EBITDA_Full_Timeline---2eb8e487-2677-47a8-ab9c-0db52dc9bc13.xlsx`

Period reviewed: Dec 2024 to Jun 2026, 19 months.

## Executive Verdict

Lux Oasis Holiday Homes has not yet been a self-sustaining operating business on the sublease model.

The business generated real revenue and proved some high-season potential, but the fixed-cost structure was too heavy. Rent alone consumed almost all documented revenue across the full timeline, leaving overhead and property opex uncovered. The result is a large negative EBITDA even before excluded capex.

This supports the strategic shift away from fixed-rent subleasing and toward revenue-management / revenue-share structures where Lux Oasis earns from performance without carrying the full rent and setup risk.

## Monthly Pattern

Only three months were EBITDA-positive:

- Jan 2025: AED +596
- Feb 2025: AED +2,961
- Dec 2025: AED +10,602

Dec 2025 is the proof that high-season revenue can work, but one strong month does not offset the fixed-cost losses across the year.

The weakest reliable operating period was after the second fixed-rent unit entered the cost base:

- Jun 2025 to May 2026 revenue: AED 215,914
- Rent: AED 247,999
- Overhead: AED 95,800
- EBITDA: AED -181,864
- Rent as % of revenue: 114.9%

That period shows the core issue clearly: adding fixed rent before revenue density was proven made the cash drag worse.

## Break-Even Reality

With property opex modelled at 25% of revenue:

- One-unit fixed cost base around AED 18,533/month needs about AED 24,711/month revenue to break even.
- Two-unit fixed cost base around AED 28,717/month needs about AED 38,289/month revenue to break even.
- Post-Murjan 3 one-unit fixed base around AED 16,383/month needs about AED 21,844/month revenue to break even.

The business average was AED 18,556/month across the full period and AED 20,668/month excluding incomplete months. That is below the two-unit break-even and barely around the reduced one-unit break-even before considering weak months.

## What Worked

- There was real demand and real revenue.
- Dec 2025 proved that the asset can produce strong high-season income.
- Murjan 3 showed that a unit can be profitable at certain points.
- Direct/Stripe revenue appeared, which means non-OTA revenue is possible.
- The business has operational learning, supplier stack, pricing tools and financial evidence now.

## What Failed

- Fixed rent was too high relative to annualised revenue.
- The business scaled fixed obligations before proving stable monthly contribution.
- Overhead became too heavy for one or two units.
- The second unit added rent and setup pressure before enough demand was captured.
- Revenue data is fragmented across Airbnb, Booking.com, Stripe, VAT workbooks and monthly files, which makes management harder.
- Booking.com and channel data gaps mean the business could not always diagnose performance cleanly in real time.

## Strategic Conclusion

The issue is not simply occupancy, ADR or better listing tweaks. Those help, but they do not fix a structure where rent consumes most revenue.

The right strategic move is:

1. Reduce or avoid fixed-rent exposure.
2. Move toward revenue-share / revenue-management structures.
3. Earn from upside while owners carry property rent/ownership risk.
4. Keep fixed overhead light until recurring retained profit supports it.
5. Use ORL-style reporting and revenue management as a product layer for Lux Oasis and external operators.

## Next Operating Rules

- Do not take another fixed-rent/sublease unit unless the unit can show a realistic base-case retained EBITDA after rent, direct costs and overhead allocation.
- For any sublease, require a conservative break-even plan, low-season downside plan and cash buffer.
- Prioritise revenue-share deals where Lux Oasis has no fixed rent obligation.
- Use retained EBITDA per unit, not top-line revenue or occupancy, as the property #2/#3 gate.
- Keep staffing / partner percentage tied to Lux Oasis retained profit, not gross revenue or full property profit.
- Build direct booking and Google Business Profile traffic, but do not let marketing hide weak unit economics.
