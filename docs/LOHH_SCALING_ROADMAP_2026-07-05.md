# Lux Oasis Holiday Homes Scaling Roadmap

Created: 2026-07-05  
Business: Lux Oasis Holiday Homes LLC  
Status: internal operating roadmap, not legal final  
Purpose: define how Lux Oasis Holiday Homes scales from one managed property into a structured, profitable, team-run holiday-home management company.

## 1. North Star

Lux Oasis Holiday Homes must become a business system, not a founder-dependent side operation.

Primary 2026 target:

- AED 50,000/month true operating profit after property costs, owner payouts, partner shares, company overhead, tools and staffing.
- Retain 50% inside the business.
- Support roughly AED 25,000/month Damiano draw only after profit is verified and cash buffers are protected.

Scaling principle:

- Unit count matters only if units are profitable, operationally manageable and cash-safe.
- The business should prioritise strong revenue-share units, then hybrid structures, then selective subleases only where conservative returns justify the risk.
- JBR, Palm and proven Dubai Marina buildings remain the first operating focus because Lux Oasis has existing knowledge and credibility there.

## 2. Current Starting Point

Current known baseline:

- Live managed portfolio: 1 property.
- Working overhead baseline: about AED 6,000/month before new fixed hires.
- Current example average from Murjan 2 / Murjan 3: about AED 2,525/month Lux Oasis retained profit per unit before partner share.
- With 25% acquisition-plus-operations partner share, current-style unit contribution falls to about AED 1,894/month.
- Scaling software and operations tools may add roughly AED 100-200 per unit/month at larger portfolio size.

Important implication:

- If units are average and partner-heavy, the portfolio needs many more units to reach founder draw goals.
- Strong unit selection and tight partner economics are not optional.

## 3. Scaling Stages

### Stage 1 - Proof Base

Target: 1-5 units.

Goal:

- prove repeatable owner acquisition;
- prove unit economics;
- prove launch checklist;
- prove cleaning/readiness control;
- prove owner reporting;
- stop Damiano from being the only operating brain.

Main work:

- property #2 acquisition;
- JBR/Palm/Marina owner and agent routes;
- Syed-style acquisition/ops partner trial;
- owner-acquisition marketing page and lead intake;
- basic SOPs for onboarding, cleaning, guest service and owner reporting.

Hiring rule:

- no heavy fixed payroll yet;
- use variable unit-level partner upside only where it creates properties or removes work.

### Stage 2 - Controlled Growth

Target: 5-10 units.

Goal:

- create a small but real portfolio;
- establish operating rhythm;
- prove that a junior operations person or partner can remove founder workload;
- standardise the owner onboarding and guest operations system.

Main work:

- weekly acquisition pipeline review;
- signed-unit launch board;
- standard owner report pack;
- cleaning and maintenance vendor bench;
- revenue-management rhythm through PriceLabs / Oasis Revenue Lab;
- Control Tower weekly scorecard.

Hiring rule:

- consider junior operations/property-management help only when portfolio profit covers the all-in cost with buffer.
- planning placeholder: AED 7,000-9,000/month depending on role and visa/insurance.

### Stage 3 - Managed Portfolio

Target: 10-20 units.

Goal:

- make Lux Oasis a real operating company with clear roles, not ad hoc founder response.
- split acquisition, operations, guest experience and owner reporting.
- start building cluster control by area.

Main work:

- assigned portfolio owner per cluster;
- revenue review by unit;
- weekly guest-risk and owner-risk review;
- stricter service standards;
- monthly property P&L;
- owner renewal and referral process.

Hiring rule:

- one full-time or near-full-time operations/property manager becomes more realistic if cash and workload justify it.
- variable acquisition partners can continue, but economics must remain tied to unit-specific Lux Oasis retained profit.

### Stage 4 - Partner Operator Network

Target: 20+ units.

Goal:

- scale through trained acquisition and cluster operators while Lux Oasis keeps brand standards, contracts, pricing governance and reporting.

This is not yet a public franchise offer.

Partner operator network controls:

- partner certification/training;
- territory or building cluster rules only after legal review;
- unit-level economics;
- no passive forever commission;
- weekly pipeline and performance reporting;
- owner and guest-service standards;
- non-circumvention and confidentiality;
- step-down rules when a partner stops contributing.

### Stage 5 - Licence / Franchise-Like Model

Target: only after the operating system is proven.

Possible later model:

- Lux Oasis brand licence;
- operator certification;
- tech/reporting fee;
- revenue-share or management-fee split;
- legal agreement;
- compliance and quality audit.

Do not sell this now. Use "Partner Operator Network" internally until legal and operational proof exists.

## 4. Acquisition Process

### Source Channels

1. Warm network and LinkedIn.
2. Property Finder / Bayut / Dubizzle forwarded listings.
3. Owner-acquisition website and SEO.
4. Google Ads and Meta owner funnel after approval.
5. Agent referral relationships.
6. Acquisition/ops partners like Syed if they prove real access.
7. Existing owner and guest referral routes.
8. ORL / Conviva operator network later as cross-business credibility.

### Lead Intake

Every potential unit must capture:

- source;
- building;
- unit type;
- furnishing status;
- rent or owner expectation;
- available date;
- current photos/listing link;
- owner/agent contact;
- deal type: revenue-share, hybrid, sublease, referral;
- estimated setup cost;
- expected gross revenue;
- expected Lux Oasis retained profit;
- operational complexity;
- launch timeline;
- next action and owner.

### Unit Scoring

Score every lead before external commitment:

- Location fit: JBR, Palm, proven Marina first.
- Unit appeal: layout, view, furnishing, building, guest demand.
- Economics: expected revenue, owner split, rent risk, setup cost, payback.
- Cash risk: cheques, deposits, furnishing, permits, launch spend.
- Operations risk: access, building rules, cleaning, maintenance, distance.
- Owner fit: realistic, responsive, not overpromising.
- Strategic fit: does it build proof in a target cluster?

Simple decision:

- A: pursue immediately.
- B: keep warm / request missing data.
- C: park.
- D: reject.

### Approval Gates

COO agent can prepare:

- research;
- unit scoring;
- outreach drafts;
- owner notes;
- revenue estimates;
- tracker updates;
- internal recommendations.

Damiano approval required before:

- sending external owner/agent messages unless an exact campaign/template is already approved;
- signing contracts;
- making revenue promises;
- changing prices/listings;
- committing spend;
- paying deposits;
- offering visa/salary/legal terms;
- accepting sublease rent risk.

## 9. Partner Economics

Default structure:

- Acquisition-only: 20% of Lux Oasis retained net profit from units personally sourced/helped closed.
- Acquisition plus active operations: 25% of Lux Oasis retained net profit from units personally sourced, closed, onboarded and actively managed.
- Proven A-to-Z operator: up to 30% only after proof.

Rules:

- always calculated on Lux Oasis retained net profit, not full property profit;
- paid only after revenue is collected and owner/direct costs are settled;
- unit-specific, not company-wide;
- no passive forever commission;
- operations-level share exists only while the partner actively performs operations;
- step-down or expiry if contribution stops.

60-day trial gate for Syed-style candidates:

- 5-10 real owner/agent routes;
- 1-2 units at serious owner-interest/economics/viewing/document stage;
- weekly pipeline report;
- no visa/support or long-term economics before proof where legally possible.

## 10. Hiring Roadmap

### Business Development / Acquisition Figure

Syed or a similar business-development figure is part of the scaling roadmap, but should be treated as a proof-gated acquisition/operations partner first, not as a guaranteed fixed employee.

Purpose of the role:

- bring owner, landlord, agent and investor routes;
- help source revenue-share or approved hybrid units;
- help move deals from conversation to viewing, economics, documents and signature;
- support onboarding and operations only if clearly assigned;
- reduce Damiano's daily workload rather than creating more follow-up.

Correct first structure:

- 60-day commercial proof period;
- no automatic visa/support before proof where legally possible;
- no fixed salary unless separately approved;
- weekly pipeline report;
- target 5-10 real owner/agent routes;
- target 1-2 units reaching serious owner-interest, economics, viewing or document stage.

Step-up logic:

- If he only introduces a lead and disappears: referral or acquisition-only economics.
- If he sources and helps close: acquisition-only partner, around 20% of Lux Oasis retained net profit from that unit.
- If he sources, closes, onboards and actively manages: acquisition-plus-operations partner, around 25%.
- If he proves genuine A-to-Z performance across multiple units: possible step-up toward 30%, but only after proof.

This role is important because Lux Oasis needs a repeatable growth engine. It is also risky if the business gives visa, title, salary or high percentage before real property access is proven.

### First Hire Type

Best first hire is not pure admin.

Best profile:

- BD plus operations;
- can source owner/agent routes;
- can help launch and operate units;
- can reduce Damiano workload;
- understands Dubai property basics.

### Hiring Gates

Hire or sponsor only when:

- role removes a real bottleneck;
- cash buffer remains protected;
- portfolio profit can support cost;
- candidate has proven access or operational usefulness;
- tasks and KPIs are written;
- approval boundaries are clear.

### Visa / Sponsorship Control

Planning:

- normal cost range: AED 8,000-10,000 per sponsored person;
- stressed cost range: AED 12,000;
- sponsor one serious person first only after proof;
- second person only after signed pipeline or proof;
- no 3+ sponsored people from current cash before revenue proves it.

## 11. Code Of Conduct And Disciplinary Code

This section is an internal operating standard. It is not a legal employment handbook until reviewed by a UAE employment/legal professional.

### Behaviour Standards

Every team member, partner, VA, cleaner, maintenance vendor and operator must:

- protect guest safety and comfort;
- protect owner trust;
- protect Lux Oasis reputation;
- report issues honestly and early;
- never hide mistakes;
- follow written tasks and SOPs;
- respect guest, owner and company confidentiality;
- avoid unauthorised promises;
- use company channels and trackers where instructed;
- confirm task completion with evidence where required.

### Prohibited Conduct

Serious issues include:

- lying about task completion;
- hiding guest, owner, maintenance, access or payment problems;
- sending unauthorised external messages;
- making owner income guarantees;
- changing prices, settings, bookings or refunds without approval;
- sharing guest/owner/private business information;
- taking owners, leads or suppliers outside Lux Oasis;
- using Lux Oasis contacts, data or templates for personal deals;
- disrespectful guest or owner communication;
- repeated no-show or late response on critical operations;
- poor cleaning/readiness confirmation without evidence;
- accepting side payments or commissions without disclosure;
- damaging property, keys, access devices or inventory through negligence.

### Disciplinary Levels

Level 1: Coaching

Use for:

- first minor process mistake;
- unclear reporting;
- small admin errors.

Action:

- explain the issue;
- restate the SOP;
- record the correction;
- monitor next completion.

Level 2: Written Warning

Use for:

- repeated minor mistakes;
- missed deadline without update;
- incomplete handover;
- missing photos/evidence after request;
- poor owner/guest follow-up.

Action:

- written warning;
- exact improvement required;
- deadline;
- consequence if repeated.

Level 3: Final Warning / Role Restriction

Use for:

- repeated failure after written warning;
- guest/owner risk;
- serious operational miss;
- failure to escalate urgent issues;
- unapproved communication that creates business risk.

Action:

- final warning;
- remove from sensitive tasks;
- require approval before action;
- reduce role or pause unit assignment.

Level 4: Termination / Removal From Partner Pool

Use for:

- dishonesty;
- theft/fraud;
- data/privacy breach;
- non-circumvention breach;
- unauthorised money, pricing, refund, contract or owner commitment;
- repeated critical operational failure;
- reputation damage;
- refusal to follow written instructions.

Action:

- terminate or remove from task/partner pool where legally and contractually allowed;
- collect company materials/access;
- handover files, contacts, keys and live tasks;
- protect guests, owners and operations first.

### Incident Process

For every issue:

1. Record what happened.
2. Identify source evidence: message, photo, tracker, guest thread, owner message.
3. Classify risk: low, medium, high.
4. Protect guest/owner first.
5. Decide coaching/warning/final/removal.
6. Update tracker and Control Tower if material.
7. Add SOP correction if the process caused the issue.

### Immediate Escalation Items

Escalate immediately to Damiano / COO system:

- guest cannot access property;
- safety issue;
- police/security/building management issue;
- owner complaint;
- refund/payment dispute;
- bad review threat;
- property damage;
- cleaner no-show near check-in;
- DTCM/building compliance issue;
- unauthorised external commitment;
- suspected dishonesty or data leak.
